# Statement of Work - Municipal Digital Services Assessment

## 1. Project Objectives

The primary objective is to modernize the city's building permit and inspection processes to enable:
- Reduced permit turnaround time from 6-8 weeks to under 2 weeks
- Improved citizen satisfaction through digital service delivery
- Streamlined internal workflows for permit staff, reviewers, and inspectors
- Elimination of paper-based processes across permitting and inspections

## 2. Scope

### In Scope
- Assessment of current permitting, inspection, and citizen service processes
- Future state process design for digital permit application and tracking
- Vendor evaluation for permitting and inspection management platforms
- Implementation roadmap with phasing and cost estimates
- Benchmarking against comparable municipalities

### Out of Scope
- Actual platform implementation and deployment
- Staff training and change management execution
- Ongoing support, maintenance, or managed services
- Custom software development
- Non-permit city services (utilities, parks, public works)

## 3. Deliverables

### 3.1 Current State Assessment Report
Comprehensive analysis of existing permitting and inspection workflows, systems inventory, staff interviews, and citizen experience mapping. Includes identification of bottlenecks, redundancies, and pain points.
**Due:** Week 4

### 3.2 Future State Process Design
Detailed process maps for digitized permit application, review routing, inspection scheduling, and citizen communication. Includes integration requirements and service level targets.
**Due:** Week 8

### 3.3 Vendor Recommendation
Evaluation of permitting and inspection management platforms against city requirements. Includes scoring matrix, cost comparison, and implementation complexity assessment.
**Due:** Week 10

### 3.4 Implementation Roadmap
Phased approach to transitioning from current to future state, including timeline, resource requirements, budget estimates, dependencies, and risk mitigation strategies.
**Due:** Week 12

## 4. Success Criteria

- Assessment report accepted by CIO and city manager
- Future state design reviewed and endorsed by permits and inspections leadership
- Vendor recommendation achieves consensus among evaluation committee
- Roadmap provides sufficient detail for fiscal year budget request

## 5. Assumptions

- City will provide timely access to permit staff, reviewers, and inspectors for interviews and observation
- Existing process documentation and system access will be made available
- An internal project lead will be assigned to coordinate city-side logistics
- Implementation phase will be a separate engagement pending city council budget approval

## 6. Constraints

- Project duration: 12 weeks
- Budget: $180,000
- Team size: 3 consultants (1 engagement lead, 1 process analyst, 1 technology analyst)
- Recommendations must align with next fiscal year planning cycle (budget request due October)
